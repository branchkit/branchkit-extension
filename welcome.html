Getting started with BranchKit Browser

Navigate any website without a mouse — keyboard first, voice optional.

Start here

Every link, button, and field gets a short badge. Press F on any page to start clicking by keyboard, then type a badge's letters to activate it — that's the whole idea.

  1. Press F → the badges highlight — their borders brighten to show they're listening (and the badges appear if you don't already keep them on)
  2. Type a badge (e.g. as) → it activates
  3. A Capital opens it in a new tab; Esc exits

Forgot a shortcut? Press ?

On any page, press ? to open the full list of shortcuts and voice phrases — it's always one key away. You can also reach it from the Help button in the toolbar popup.

Add voice — install BranchKit

Everything above is keyboard-only and needs nothing else. But install the BranchKit desktop app — with its Browser plugin enabled — and the extension connects to it automatically, which activates voice control. Every badge becomes speakable: say its codeword to click, type, or open a link, and drive scrolling, tabs, and the rest by voice — no keyboard needed. The popup shows a Voice indicator once it's connected.

Works on any site, with no account. Nothing leaves your browser — page data is used only to draw badges, and only reaches the optional desktop app on your own machine.
